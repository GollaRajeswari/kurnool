Maha Shivaratri

  • Maha Shivaratri is one of the major festivals that take place in Kurnool.
  • Devotees flock Sri Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple at Srisailam which is close by.
  • It's decorated with flowers inside the temple full of people who throng to join all night-long prayers, fasting and vigils.
  • The temple compound looks stunningly beautiful especially at this time when devotion fills the air.

Ugadi

  • Ugadi is the Telugu New Year celebrated with great zeal in Kurnool.
  • Celebrated typically in March or April signifies new beginning for every living being on earth.
  • People are busy cleaning their houses and adorning their doorways with green mango leaves as well as colorful rangolis.
  • Such dishes like Ugadi Pachadi which incorporate all flavors; sweet,sour,salt,bitter & spicy are made denoting various experiences of life. Some traditional dances, music, cultural programmes add vibrancy to the atmosphere.

Dussehra

  • Dasara festival (also called Vijayadashami) is a grand affair here.
  • Children burn firecrackers while dressed up like Lord Rama.
  • Most importantly there's a ten-day celebration period fill it with colorful processions around town decorated and adorned streets.
  • This culminates into Ramleela a very dramatic presentation of Ramayana done so worshipfully and passionately.

Muharram

  • Kurnool has a large Muslim community, and Muharram is one of their important festivals here.
  • The festival is celebrated to remember the martyrdom of Imam Hussain who was the grandson of Prophet Muhammad.
  • Participants are seen in a procession carrying tazias (replicas of the martyrs) as well as prayers and religious gatherings.
  • This period is very solemn and reflective.
